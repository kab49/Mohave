intro: "A low, rounded shrub characteristic of the Mojave, featuring bright green foliage and dense clusters of small, yellow flower heads. Amphipappus fremontii is a monotypic genus, meaning it is the only species within its genus. It is highly specialized for the harsh conditions of the Mojave Desert, often found growing in the company of creosote bush and bursage. The common name chaffbush refers to the scale-like or chaffy nature of the pappus found on the seeds."
