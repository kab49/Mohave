intro: "A ubiquitous and hardy winter annual, recognized by its fern-like foliage and distinctive long, needle-like fruits that resemble a bird's beak. Erodium cicutarium is one of the first plants to bloom in the desert spring. While non-native, it has become deeply integrated into the desert ecology. Its seed dispersal mechanism is a marvel of biological engineering: when dry, the tail of the seed twists into a tight spiral, and when moistened by dew or rain, it uncoils, effectively drilling the seed into the soil."
