Ethnobotanical: A notable subspecies is the totora, Schoenoplectus californicus subsp. tatora. This is famous for making up the floating islands on which the Uros people of Lake Titicaca dwell, as well as occurring on isolated Easter Island in the Pacific. Boats have also been made from this plant by many cultures, including the Caballito de totora in Peru for over 3,000 years.
